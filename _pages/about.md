## Devergnas Lab

The Devergnas Lab studies neural circuits underlying epilepsy, sleep
dysfunction, and neuromodulation using electrophysiology and
nonhuman primate models.

Our work combines systems neuroscience, deep brain stimulation,
and translational neurology to better understand the mechanisms
of seizures and develop new therapeutic approaches.

---

## Principal Investigator

**Annaelle Devergnas, PhD**  
Assistant Professor  
Department of Neurology, Emory University  
Emory National Primate Research Center

Dr. Devergnas is an electrophysiologist specializing in neural
oscillations, neuromodulation, and epilepsy research.

She trained in France under the supervision of **Pr. Alim-Louis
Benabid**, a pioneer of deep brain stimulation. In 2009 she joined
the laboratory of **Thomas Wichmann, MD** at Emory University to
study oscillatory activity in Parkinsonism using MPTP-treated
nonhuman primate models.

In 2016 she became director of the **Neuromodulation Lab** at the
Emory National Primate Research Center.

Her laboratory has developed a nonhuman primate model of
on-demand neocortical and temporal lobe seizures and adapted
telemetry systems to record EEG and deep brain activity in freely
moving animals.

## Research Themes

### Neural Circuit Mechanisms of Epilepsy
Investigating the electrophysiological pathways underlying
seizure initiation and propagation in primate models.

### Neuromodulation and Deep Brain Stimulation
Developing neuromodulation strategies including deep brain
stimulation and pharmacological interventions such as
T-type calcium channel blockers.

### Seizure Comorbidities
Studying depression, cognitive impairment, and **SUDEP**
(sudden unexpected death in epilepsy) associated with seizures.

### Sleep and Epilepsy
Understanding the interaction between seizures and sleep
disorders to improve treatment strategies.
